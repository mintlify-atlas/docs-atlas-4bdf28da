The Executive Dashboard is your agency's central nerve system. It surfaces the metrics that matter most — revenue, margin, project velocity, and pipeline value — alongside a real-time feed of every significant action taken across Zenn OS. Everything on this page updates live, and you can hide financial figures instantly if you're sharing your screen.

## KPI cards

The four KPI cards run across the top of the dashboard. Each card shows a large current value, a trend indicator (green arrow up, red arrow down), a period-over-period change label, and a mini sparkline plotting the last six data points.

| Metric | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Gross Revenue** | Total invoiced amount across all projects in the current period |
| **Net Profit** | Gross revenue minus all outgoing payouts and expenses |
| **Project Velocity** | Rolling average days to complete a milestone across active projects |
| **Active Pipeline** | Sum of total contract values for all active projects |

When **Sensitivity Mode** is on, all four values display as `••••••` and the sparklines are hidden entirely. The mode activates and deactivates with a single click on the **Sensitivity Mode** button in the header. The button border turns red while the mode is active.

## P&L chart

The P&L chart occupies the left two-thirds of the main content row. It is an area chart showing **Receivables** (solid line) against **Payables** (dashed line) month by month. Hovering over any data point shows a tooltip with exact dollar values.

Use the toggle buttons in the chart header to switch between:

- `monthly` — last six calendar months (default)
- `quarterly` — last four quarters
- `yearly` — annual totals

<Info>
The chart data comes from reconciled transactions in the Financial Ledger. Unreconciled transactions are included in the Payables line but flagged separately in the Ledger view.
</Info>

## Live activity feed

The live feed panel occupies the right third of the main content row. It streams the most recent `ActivityEvent` records, each tagged with a category and a timestamp.

**Category color coding:**

- `financial` — green dot (payment confirmed, invoice issued)
- `legal` — red dot (contract signed, IP transferred)
- `production` — blue dot (milestone submitted or approved)
- `access` — red dot when combined with `isRedFlag: true`
- `system` — grey dot (new inquiry received, user onboarded)

Events with `isRedFlag: true` render with a subtle red background tint and a warning triangle icon. These indicate actions that break expected workflow — for example, manually unlocking Vault access without milestone dependencies being met.

<Warning>
Red flag events require your attention. They are not blocked automatically — Zenn OS logs them and surfaces them here so you can follow up.
</Warning>

## Project health panel

The project health panel lists all projects with `status: 'active'`. Each row shows:

- A colored status dot: **green** (on track), **amber** (at risk), **red** (critical)
- The project name and client name
- A completion percentage and a proportionally filled progress bar

Clicking any project row navigates directly to the **Dependency Architect** with that project pre-selected, so you can inspect the milestone chain without leaving the dashboard context.

The **Dependency Architect** link in the panel header navigates to `/admin/dependency` for a full graph view.

## Hot leads panel

The hot leads panel lists all leads where `temperature` is `'warm'` or `'hot'` and `stage` is not `'converted'` or `'lost'`. For each lead you see:

- A colored dot: red for `hot`, amber for `warm`
- The company name and current pipeline stage
- The estimated budget (rounded to the nearest thousand)
- A temperature badge

Leads flagged with `isSpamFlag: true` display a warning triangle instead of the temperature dot, alerting you to review before engaging.

At the bottom of the panel, a **Burn Rate Alert** appears when active projects have logged revision rounds beyond their contracted scope. Clicking **View Pipeline** in the panel header navigates to the full Inquiry Inbox at `/admin/intake`.

## Quick-Call Alert button

The **Quick-Call Alert** button in the dashboard header sends an immediate push notification to any team member you choose. It opens a modal where you type a brief reason for the call, select the recipient, and dispatch the alert. Use this when a milestone is at risk and you need a response faster than a nudge would provide.

<Tip>
Combine the Quick-Call Alert with the Personnel Directory's reliability score to decide who to contact first. Team members with lower `reliabilityScore` values and higher `nudgeCount` values may need a direct call rather than a nudge.
</Tip>
